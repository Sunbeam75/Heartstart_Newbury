Heartstart was setup in 1996 by the charity British Heart Foundation, and thanks to their work over 3.5 million to date have attended a Heartstart course. The “Heartstart Newbury” is one of the many schemes administrated by the British Heart Foundation which provide this FREE CPR (cardiopulmonary resuscitation) and emergency life saving training to the community. “Heartstart Newbury” was setup with accredited instructors in August 2015 to serve Newbury and the surrounding area.

About the course

The “Heartstart Newbury” course lasts two hours. On the course you will learn about:


  1. dealing with an unconscious casualty

  2. the recovery position

  3. the signs and symptoms of a heart attack

  4. recognising cardiac arrest

  5. performing CPR (cardiopulmonary resuscitation)

  6. dealing with choking

  7. dealing with serious bleeding
